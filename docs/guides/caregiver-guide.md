## 1. Welcome

If you are reading this, you are already taking a meaningful step. Caring for a child and wanting to understand their world better — noticing things, asking questions, seeking knowledge — is one of the most important things a caregiver can do.

The Ambitious about Autism platform has been designed with you in mind. It gives you:

- **Structured training** to help you recognise early developmental patterns associated with autism.
- **Observation tools** to log what you notice about a child's behaviour over time.
- **AI-assisted summaries** to help you make sense of those observations before a healthcare appointment.
- **Printable reports** to bring along to meetings with your GP, health visitor, or a SENCO at school.

This guide walks you through each part of the platform. You do not need any specialist knowledge to get started — just open it, take your time, and go at your own pace.

## 2. Getting Started

### Creating an account

Visit the platform at https://asd-training-app-v2.vercel.app and click **Sign up** or **Register**.

You will be asked to provide:

- **Your full name**
- **Your email address**
- **A password** (choose something memorable but secure)
- **Your role** — select **Caregiver**

Choosing the Caregiver role unlocks the parts of the platform described in this guide: ASD training, child observation tracking, AI insights, and reports.

You can also sign in using your **Google account** or **Microsoft account** if your organisation has enabled those options. If you are unsure whether this applies to you, ask your organisation's administrator.

### Logging in

Once registered, visit the platform and click **Sign in**. The login page has a toggle at the top to switch between **Email & Password** and **Single Sign-On**:

- **Email & Password** — enter your email address and password, then click **Sign in**. If you have forgotten your password, click **Forgot password** and a reset link will be sent to your email.
- **Single Sign-On** — if your organisation supports it, click **Google** or **Microsoft** to sign in with your existing account.

## 3. Your Dashboard

When you sign in, you land on your **Dashboard**. Think of this as your home base. Here is what you will find:

### Welcome message

The platform greets you by first name. Below the greeting is a short summary of where things stand.

### Important disclaimer banner

As a Caregiver, you will see an important disclaimer banner reminding you that this platform is an observation and training tool — not a diagnostic tool. This banner is shown only to Caregiver accounts and does not appear for other roles.

### Announcements

If your organisation or the platform administrators have posted any important notices — new training content, reminders, or updates — they appear here at the top of your dashboard.

### Your progress at a glance

As a Caregiver, your dashboard shows three summary cards that are specific to your role:

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| Lessons completed | How many lessons you have finished out of the total |
| Children tracked | How many child profiles you have added |
| Total observations | The total number of observations you have logged across all children |

The children and observations cards are caregiver-specific features — other roles on the platform do not see them.

### Training progress panel

A more detailed view of each training module shows how many lessons you have completed per module, and a link to continue or start each one.

### Quick actions

Shortcut buttons let you jump straight to **Add a child** or continue your training. The "Add a child" action is a caregiver-specific feature.

### Recent observations

If you have logged any observations, the five most recent ones appear here for a quick reminder of what you last recorded.

## 4. Training Modules

The **ASD Training** section (accessible from the left-hand sidebar) is the learning heart of the platform. It contains five modules covering what caregivers need to know about recognising and documenting early developmental patterns.

### Finding your training

Click **ASD Training** in the sidebar. You will see a progress banner showing how many lessons you have completed overall, followed by cards for each module.

### How the modules work

**Modules unlock in order.** You need to complete all the lessons in a module before the next one becomes available. This is intentional — each module builds on the previous one.

Each module card shows:
- The module title and a short description
- How many lessons it contains and how many you have completed
- A **Start** or **Continue** button

### Working through a lesson

Click a module card to see the list of lessons inside it. Click any available lesson to open it. A lesson page contains:

1. **Lesson content** — reading material written in clear, accessible language. Some lessons may also include a video.
2. **A reflection prompt** — before the quiz, you are invited to pause and think: have you noticed any of the behaviours described in this lesson with a child in your care? There is no right or wrong answer — it is just a moment to connect the learning to real life.
3. **A knowledge check quiz** — a short set of questions to confirm your understanding.

### Completing a lesson

After reading the lesson material, click **Start quiz**. Work through each question and submit your answers. Once the quiz is complete, the lesson is marked as done and your progress is saved automatically. You can then move to the next lesson.

If you close the browser mid-lesson, do not worry — your completed lessons are already saved and you can pick up where you left off.

### About the training disclaimer

At the bottom of the Training Hub, you will see this reminder:

> *"These modules are designed to help caregivers and early years practitioners recognise and document developmental patterns. Completing this training does not qualify you to diagnose ASD. Always refer concerns to a qualified healthcare professional."*

Please keep this in mind as you work through the content. The goal of this training is to make you a more confident, more informed observer — not to give you the tools to make a clinical judgement.

## 5. Child Observations

The **Child Observations** section (in the sidebar) is where you log what you notice about a child's behaviour. Over time, these observations build into a picture that you can review, share, and use as the basis of a conversation with healthcare professionals.

### Adding a child profile

Before you can log any observations, you need to create a profile for each child you are tracking.

1. Click **Child Observations** in the sidebar, or use the **Add a child** quick action on the dashboard.
2. Click the **Add child** button (top right).
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Name** — the child's first name, or a nickname if you prefer.
   - **Date of birth** — used to calculate the child's age, which is useful context for observations.
   - **Notes** (optional) — any general background notes about the child, for example their current setting, relevant history, or anything else you want to remember.
4. Save the profile.

You can add as many children as you need. Each child has their own profile page with separate observations, charts, and AI insights.

### Logging an observation

To log a new observation, open a child's profile and click **Log observation** (top right). A form will slide in asking for the following details:

**Date**
When did you observe this behaviour? Defaults to today.

**Domain**
Which area of development does this behaviour relate to? There are three domains:

| Domain | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **Social Communication** | How the child communicates, makes eye contact, responds to their name, engages with others, and develops language |
| **Behaviour and Play** | Routines, repetitive behaviours, play patterns, transitions between activities, and specific interests |
| **Sensory Responses** | How the child reacts to sensory input — sounds, lights, textures, smells, and physical touch |

**Behaviour type**
Once you select a domain, a list of specific behaviours appears. Choose the one that best describes what you observed. Examples include:

- *Limited response to name* (Social Communication)
- *Repetitive play patterns* (Behaviour and Play)
- *High sensitivity to noise* (Sensory Responses)

**Frequency**
How often have you seen this behaviour recently?

| Option | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Rare | Observed once or twice |
| Sometimes | Observed a few times a week |
| Often | Observed daily or near-daily |

**Context**
Where did this behaviour typically occur?
- Home
- Nursery / School
- Outdoors
- Other

**Notes** (optional)
A free-text field for any additional detail you want to capture — for example, what triggered the behaviour, how the child responded, or anything else that seemed significant.

When you are happy with the details, save the observation.

### Viewing a child's profile

Each child profile has four tabs:

**Overview**
A summary of the child's total observations, how many domains have been recorded, and how many "often" behaviours have been logged. Also shows the child's date of birth and any notes you added. A bar chart shows the relative count of observations across the three domains.

**4-Week Tracker**
A table of all observations logged in the last four weeks, sorted by most recent first. You can delete individual observations from here if needed.

**Charts**
Visual charts showing weekly trends across the three domains over the last four weeks. These charts are particularly useful for spotting whether a behaviour is becoming more or less frequent over time.

**AI Insights**
This is where you can generate an AI-assisted summary of the child's observations. See [Section 6](#6-ai-insights) for full details.

### Editing and deleting children

To delete a child profile and all their associated observations, open the child's profile page and click the delete icon (bin) in the top right. You will be asked to confirm. This action cannot be undone.

## 6. AI Insights

The **AI Insights** tab on each child's profile uses Google Gemini, an AI model, to help you make sense of the observations you have logged.

### What it is — and what it is not

> **Please read this carefully.**
>
> AI Insights are a tool to help you organise and articulate what you have observed. They are generated by an AI that has been instructed never to suggest a diagnosis, never to speculate beyond what the data shows, and to use warm, accessible language appropriate for a caregiver.
>
> **An AI Insight report is not a diagnosis. It is not a clinical assessment. It is not a substitute for professional advice.**
>
> The purpose of this feature is to help you walk into an appointment with your GP, health visitor, or SENCO feeling prepared — with a clear summary of what you have noticed and a starting point for conversation.

### How to generate an insight

1. Open a child's profile and click the **AI Insights** tab.
2. You need at least one observation logged before an insight can be generated.
3. Click the **Generate report** button.
4. The report takes a few moments to generate. Please wait — do not close the page.

### What the report contains

The AI insight report has three sections:

**Summary**
Two or three sentences describing the overall picture of what has been observed, in plain, caregiver-friendly language. It focuses on patterns — not conclusions.

**Patterns**
A bullet-point list identifying the main behavioural patterns across the three domains. This is drawn directly from the observations you have logged.

**Recommendations**
Three or four practical next steps — including which professionals to speak to, such as your GP, health visitor, speech and language therapist, or the child's SENCO. The language is warm and encouraging.

Every report ends with the same disclaimer:
> *"These observations are for discussion with your GP, health visitor, or SENCO. This is not a diagnosis."*

### How to use your AI insight

Treat it as a conversation starter. You might:

- **Print or screenshot** the insight report before an appointment.
- **Share it with your GP or health visitor** to help describe what you have been noticing at home.
- **Bring it to a SENCO meeting** at nursery or school.
- **Use the recommendations section** to think about what kind of support to ask for.

The more observations you log — and the more consistent you are in recording frequency and context — the more useful the AI summary will be.

## 7. Reports and Charts

The **Reports** section (in the sidebar) gives you a broader view across all children, and lets you produce a printable document for healthcare appointments.

### Filters

At the top of the Reports page, two filters let you narrow down what you are looking at:

- **Child** — view all children, or select one specific child.
- **Date range** — choose from the last 7 days, 2 weeks, 4 weeks, 3 months, or 1 year.

### What the report shows

For each child in the selected date range:

- **Child header** — name, date of birth, report generated date, total observations in the period, and any caregiver notes.
- **Weekly Observation Trends chart** — a bar chart showing how many observations were logged per week across the three domains.
- **Observations by domain** — a detailed table listing every observation in the period, grouped by domain, with date, behaviour, frequency, context, and any notes.

### Printing your report

Click **Print report** at the top right. The report is formatted for printing — filters and navigation are hidden, leaving a clean document.

The printed report includes a footer:
> *"This report contains observational data only and does not constitute a diagnosis. Share this document with your GP, health visitor, or SENCO for professional assessment. Observations were recorded by a caregiver using the Ambitious about Autism tool."*

This makes the document self-explanatory if you hand it to a professional.

## 8. Virtual Classroom Sessions

Your organisation may invite you to attend live virtual training sessions. These can be held over Zoom, Microsoft Teams, or another platform.

### Seeing upcoming sessions

When you have been invited to a session, an **Upcoming Sessions** card will appear on your Dashboard showing the next session's title, date, and time at a glance.

### Viewing all your sessions

Click **Sessions** in the left-hand sidebar to see the full list of sessions you have been invited to, along with their status (Upcoming, In Progress, Completed, or Cancelled).

### Joining a session

When a session is ready to begin, a **Join** button will appear next to it. Click **Join** to open the meeting link — this will take you directly into the Zoom or Teams meeting, or to whatever platform your organisation is using.

### Recordings

After a session has taken place, your organisation's admin may add a recording link. If a recording is available, a **Watch recording** link will appear on the session in your Sessions list.

### Hosting a session

If your organisation's administrator has assigned you as a **host** for a session, you will have additional options when you open that session:

- **Meeting link** — add or update the link that attendees will use to join.
- **Attendance** — check off which attendees were present once the session is under way or has finished.
- **Recording** — add a recording URL after the session ends so attendees can watch it back.

## 9. Settings

The **Settings** page (bottom of the sidebar) lets you view your account details and manage your data.

### Your account

Displays your name, email address, and role (Caregiver).

### Data and privacy

Your data is stored securely in a PostgreSQL database hosted on Microsoft Azure in the EU. The platform stores your name, email, child profiles, and observations. No data is sold or shared with third parties.

The only third-party processing that occurs is when you generate an AI insight — your observation data is sent to Google Gemini for that specific request. Google does not retain this data.

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to access, correct, and erase your data. To request a copy of your data, contact: privacy@ambitiousaboutautism.org.uk

### Deleting your account

If you wish to delete your account and all associated data (child profiles, observations, AI insights, and training progress), scroll to the **Danger Zone** section on the Settings page. Type `DELETE` in the confirmation field and click the delete button.

This action is permanent and cannot be reversed.

## 10. Getting Help

### If something is not working

Try the following steps first:

1. **Refresh the page** — most loading issues resolve with a refresh.
2. **Sign out and sign back in** — if pages are not loading correctly, signing out and back in resets your session.
3. **Check your internet connection** — the platform requires an active internet connection.
4. **Try a different browser** — the platform works best on up-to-date versions of Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari.

### If you have a question about the training content

The training content is reviewed by specialists. If something in a lesson seems unclear or you are unsure how to apply it, please note your question and raise it with the professional you are already working with (your health visitor, the child's SENCO, etc.) — they will be in the best position to help you apply the learning to a specific child.

### If you are concerned about a child

If you have an urgent concern about a child's welfare or health, please contact your GP, health visitor, or call 111 (NHS non-emergency). Do not wait for AI insights or training completion before seeking professional support.

### Remembering the platform's purpose

The reminder in the sidebar is worth keeping front of mind:

> *"This tool does not diagnose. Share observations with your GP or health visitor."*

You are doing something valuable by logging what you notice and learning more. The platform is here to support that — not to replace the professionals who can truly assess and help.

### Dark mode

The platform fully supports dark mode. All interface elements — including role badges, buttons, and charts — remain clearly readable in both light and dark themes. Dark mode follows your system preference automatically.
