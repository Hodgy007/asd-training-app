## 1. Welcome

The Ambitious about Autism training platform provides structured Continuing Professional Development (CPD) for careers professionals who support autistic young people. This includes careers leaders, SENCOs, SEND co-ordinators, and anyone in a guidance or pastoral role working with autistic pupils or students.

The platform is built around four careers modules that align with the **Gatsby Benchmarks** framework for careers education. Each module combines professional development reading, practical guidance, and a knowledge check quiz. Your progress is tracked automatically so you can work through the content at your own pace, returning to pick up where you left off whenever it suits you.

**What this platform is not:** The training does not lead to a formal qualification or accreditation. It provides a structured, evidence-informed CPD resource to support and document your professional learning.

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Creating your account

1. Navigate to the platform at [https://asd-training-app-v2.vercel.app](https://asd-training-app-v2.vercel.app).
2. Select **Create account** (or follow the link from your organisation's welcome email).
3. On the registration page, choose your role. Select **Careers Professional** — described as *"I am a careers leader, SENCO, or SEND professional working with autistic young people."* This is important: choosing the correct role ensures you are shown the careers CPD content rather than the caregiver training modules.
4. Enter your full name, work email address, and a password of at least 8 characters.
5. Read and tick the privacy consent checkbox. Your data is stored securely on EU-hosted servers (Neon PostgreSQL on Azure). No data is sold or shared with third parties.
6. Select **Create account**.

You will be redirected to the sign-in page with a confirmation message. Sign in with the credentials you just created.

### 2.2 Signing in with Google or Microsoft

If your organisation uses Google Workspace or Microsoft 365, you can sign in using those credentials instead of a separate password. On the registration or sign-in page, select **Google** or **Microsoft** as appropriate. Your account must be created by an administrator before SSO sign-in will work — if you receive an "access denied" message, contact your organisation administrator.

### 2.3 Signing in

1. Go to the platform URL and select **Sign in**.
2. Enter your email address and password, then select **Sign in**.
3. If you have forgotten your password, select **Forgot password?** and enter your email address. A reset link will be sent to your inbox.

### 2.4 Your role badge

Once signed in, you will see a blue **Careers Professional** badge in the sidebar. This confirms your account has the correct role and that you have access to the careers CPD modules.

## 3. Your Dashboard

After signing in you are taken directly to your **Dashboard** at `/dashboard`. This is your home screen throughout the platform.

### 3.1 Welcome and progress summary

The top of the dashboard greets you by name and shows three summary tiles:

- **Lessons completed** — how many lessons across all your active modules you have finished, shown as a fraction of the total available (for example, *3 / 16*).
- **Children tracked** — relevant to caregiver accounts; as a Careers Professional this will show zero and can be disregarded.
- **Total observations** — similarly a caregiver feature; this will show zero for your account.

Below the tiles is a **Training Progress** panel showing each of your active modules with a per-module completion count and a quick **Start** or **Continue** link.

### 3.2 Announcements

If your organisation's administrator or the platform team has posted any announcements — for example, information about new content, events, or policy updates — these appear in a highlighted banner near the top of the dashboard. Announcements may have an expiry date and will disappear once they are no longer active.

### 3.3 Navigation

The sidebar on the left (or accessible via the menu icon on mobile) contains your main navigation links:

| Link | Where it goes |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Your home screen |
| **Careers Training** | The careers CPD training hub |
| **Settings** | Your account and privacy settings |
| **Sign out** | Signs you out of the platform |

## 4. Careers CPD Training

Select **Careers Training** in the sidebar to reach the training hub at `/careers`.

### 4.1 Overview page

At the top of the Careers Training page is a blue progress card showing your overall completion across all modules — total lessons completed, total available, and an overall percentage. Below this are cards for each of the four careers modules.

A note at the bottom of the page reads:

> *This training is designed to support careers professionals working with autistic young people under the Gatsby Benchmarks framework. Completion of all modules provides a structured evidence base for SEND-inclusive careers education practice. It does not constitute a formal qualification.*

### 4.2 The four modules

The careers CPD programme consists of four modules, which must be completed in order. Each module unlocks only after the previous one has been fully completed.

---

#### Module 1: Understanding Autism in the Careers Context

This foundational module establishes the knowledge base for autism-inclusive careers practice. It covers how autism presents in educational and workplace settings, the language and frameworks used in SEND careers guidance, and the core principles that underpin effective support for autistic young people.

---

#### Module 2: Autism-Inclusive Careers Planning

This module focuses on the careers guidance process itself — how to adapt interviews, action plans, and information resources to be genuinely inclusive. It covers person-centred approaches, supported decision-making, and how to communicate labour market information in ways that are accessible to autistic young people with varying communication styles.

---

#### Module 3: Employer Engagement and Work Experience

This module addresses the relationship between careers professionals, employers, and work experience providers. It covers how to brief employers on autism-aware practice, what adjustments to look for in work placements, and how to prepare autistic young people for workplace interactions and transitions.

---

#### Module 4: Collaboration and Referral Pathways

The final module focuses on working alongside other professionals — SEN teams, EHCPs, health and social care, and specialist employment services. It covers referral pathways, recording and evidencing outcomes, and how to build a joined-up support network around an autistic young person's career journey.

---

### 4.3 Working through a module

**To open a module**, select **Start module** (or **Continue** if you have already begun) on its card. Modules that are locked show a padlock icon and the message *"Complete previous module to unlock"*.

On the **module overview page** you will see:

- The module description and your current progress bar.
- A **Start first lesson** or **Continue learning** button that takes you straight to your next incomplete lesson.
- A full list of all lessons in the module, each showing its type (text or video) and how many quiz questions it contains. Completed lessons are marked with a green tick. Within a module, lessons must be completed in sequence — later lessons are locked until the preceding one is finished.

### 4.4 Working through a lesson

Each lesson page has three sections:

**1. Lesson content**
The main professional development content, which may be a text article with headings and structured sections, or a video lesson. Read or watch the content in full before proceeding.

**2. Reflect on your practice**
A short prompt appears after the content:

> *Before starting the quiz, consider how the ideas in this lesson apply to a young person you work with. How might you adapt your approach based on what you have just read?*

This is not submitted or recorded — it is an opportunity to consolidate your learning before the knowledge check.

**3. Knowledge check (quiz)**
Each lesson concludes with a short quiz. Select **Start quiz** when you are ready. Questions are multiple-choice. After submitting each answer you will receive immediate feedback showing whether your response was correct and a brief explanation of the answer. Work through all questions to complete the lesson.

### 4.5 Progress saving

Progress is saved automatically when you complete a quiz. You do not need to do anything manually. If you close the browser or navigate away mid-lesson before completing the quiz, the lesson will not be marked as complete and you can return to it at any time. Once a lesson is marked complete, it remains complete permanently and contributes to your overall progress percentage.

### 4.6 Reviewing completed lessons

Completed lessons and modules remain accessible. On the module overview page, select **Review** next to any completed lesson to re-read the content. On the module hub, completed modules show a **Review module** button instead of the start/continue button.

## 5. Account Settings

Select **Settings** in the sidebar to reach your account settings page at `/settings`.

### 5.1 Your account information

The account panel displays your name, email address, and your role (shown as *Careers Professional*). These are set at registration. If you need to update your name or email address, contact your organisation administrator or use the support email below.

### 5.2 Data and privacy

Your data is stored on EU-hosted servers. The platform stores your name, email address, and training progress records. No data is sold or shared with third parties.

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access, correct, and erase your data. To request a copy of your data or to make a correction, contact **privacy@ambitiousaboutautism.org.uk**.

### 5.3 Deleting your account

The **Danger Zone** section at the bottom of the settings page allows you to permanently delete your account and all associated data, including your training progress records. This action cannot be undone.

To confirm deletion, type `DELETE` into the confirmation field and select **Delete my account and all data**. You will be signed out immediately and your account will be removed.

## 6. Getting Help

**Technical issues** (login problems, pages not loading, progress not saving): contact **privacy@ambitiousaboutautism.org.uk** and include a description of the issue and the browser you are using.

**Content queries** (questions about module content or the Gatsby Benchmarks framework): speak to your line manager or organisation's SEND lead, or refer to the [Gatsby Foundation careers guidance resources](https://www.gatsby.org.uk/education/focus-areas/good-career-guidance).

**Account administration** (role changes, adding colleagues, organisational access): contact your organisation's designated platform administrator, who can manage user accounts through the admin panel.

**Privacy and data requests**: email **privacy@ambitiousaboutautism.org.uk** with your name and request.
